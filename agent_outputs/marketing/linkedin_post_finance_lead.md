If you run finance at a Lagos SME, here's a question:

How many hours did your team spend last month figuring out which `TRF FROM 080xxxxxxxx` line in your GTBank statement matched which invoice?

We asked 23 finance leads at Nigerian SMEs the same question.

Average answer: **9 hours a week.**

That's roughly ₦180,000/month of someone's salary spent on the single most boring, error-prone job in your books.

It's not their fault. It's the tooling.

→ QuickBooks and Xero were built for a world where every payment carries a clean reference. Naira bank narrations and MoMo transfers don't work that way.

→ Excel works until you cross ~200 invoices a month, then collapses under partial payments and deposits split across three weeks.

→ Custom-built reconciliation tools exist, but they cost ₦5M+ and 6 months to deploy.

We're building NovaMesh because there's a missing middle: a tool that ingests your invoices and your GTBank / Access / Zenith / MoMo statements, matches them automatically, and flags the ambiguous ones for a 30-second human review.

No integrations to wire up. No consultant. CSV in, reconciled report out.
